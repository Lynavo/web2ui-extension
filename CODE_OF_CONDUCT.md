# Code of conduct

Web2UI contributors and maintainers are expected to make participation respectful, focused, and
safe. This applies to repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, and other project
spaces.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful of different backgrounds, identities, experience levels, and viewpoints.
- Critique ideas and code with specific evidence; do not attack or demean people.
- Keep reports reproducible and remove private page content, credentials, and personal data.
- Accept maintainer decisions about scope, security boundaries, licensing, and release readiness.
- Correct mistakes promptly and help preserve a constructive technical record.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized conduct, deliberate intimidation, doxxing,
publication of private data, sustained disruption, and bad-faith manipulation are not permitted.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove content, lock conversations, reject contributions, or temporarily or
permanently restrict participation when behavior is unsafe or persistently disruptive. Enforcement
decisions should be proportionate, documented privately where appropriate, and avoid exposing the
person who raised a concern.
